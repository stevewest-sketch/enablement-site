Persona Overview

Understanding the three technical executive buyer types

CTO

Chief Technology Officer

Primary Focus

Speed, Scale, Engineering Efficiency, Time-to-Market

Key Priorities

Technical architecture, innovation roadmap, scalability, developer productivity, technical debt reduction

Budget Authority

Direct control over AI and technology initiatives. Approves strategic platform decisions.

What They Care About

API quality, system integration, technical excellence, future-proof architecture

CIO

Chief Information Officer

Primary Focus

Cost/Risk Management, Vendor Consolidation, Predictable TCO

Key Priorities

Security, compliance, operational excellence, vendor management, IT efficiency, cost control

Budget Authority

IT budget ownership and vendor relationship management. Controls enterprise software spend.

What They Care About

ROI, security certifications, compliance, vendor reliability, total cost of ownership

CDO

Chief Digital Officer

Primary Focus

Digital Experience, Omnichannel Innovation, Competitive Differentiation

Key Priorities

Customer experience transformation, digital channel optimization, innovation, competitive advantage

Budget Authority

Digital transformation initiatives and customer-facing technology investments.

What They Care About

Customer satisfaction, channel consistency, speed to market, measurable business outcomes

Action Playbook

What to do in different scenarios

🔍 No Tech Leader Relationship Yet?

Situation: You're working with CX team but haven't connected with CTO/CIO/CDO

  • Send email + one-pager to your champion
  • Ask them to forward to their tech leadership
  • Position as: "Want to make sure your tech team knows what we're delivering"
  • Use templates in Resources section below

⚠️ Competitor Already in Conversations?

Situation: You hear a competitor is talking to their CTO/CIO/CDO

  • Escalate to your manager IMMEDIATELY
  • Share one-pager with your champion
  • Brief them on key talking points
  • Request introduction to tech leadership
  • Use competitive battle cards for positioning

✅ Tech Leader in the Room?

Situation: CTO/CIO/CDO joins a call or meeting

  • Use the positioning talk track above
  • Highlight 2-3 capabilities matching their priorities
  • Focus on governance and control
  • Offer security documentation and ROI data
  • Schedule follow-up technical deep dive

🎯 Tech Leader NOT in Room?

Situation: You're in CX conversations but tech leaders aren't involved yet

  • Share one-pager with your contact
  • Suggest they forward to tech team
  • Position as value-add, not requirement
  • Offer to present to tech leadership
  • Include ROI calculator and security docs

⚡ When to Escalate

Red Flag Situations:

  • Competitor mentioned in tech leadership conversations
  • Security or compliance concerns raised by IT
  • Budget approval stalled at tech leadership level
  • Integration or architecture questions you can't answer

What to do: Immediately escalate to your manager and Solutions Engineering team. Don't try to handle complex technical objections alone.
